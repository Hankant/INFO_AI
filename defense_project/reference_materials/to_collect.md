# Suggested Papers to Collect

These are the works already represented in `../latex/references.bib`. This folder can be used to store their PDFs and your reading notes for the defense.

## Foundational Theory

- Kamenica and Gentzkow (2011), *Bayesian Persuasion*
- Bergemann and Morris (2019), *Information Design: A Unified Perspective*
- de Clippel and Zhang (2022), *Non-Bayesian Persuasion*
- de Clippel and Zhang (2020 working paper version), *Non-Bayesian Persuasion*

## Information Acquisition and Belief Updating

- Sims (2003), *Implications of Rational Inattention*
- Caplin and Dean (2015), *Revealed Preference, Rational Inattention, and Costly Information Acquisition*
- Payne, Bettman, and Johnson (1993), *The Adaptive Decision Maker*
- Enke and Zimmermann (2019), *Correlation Neglect in Belief Formation*
- Nickerson (1998), *Confirmation Bias: A Ubiquitous Phenomenon in Many Guises*
- Lord, Ross, and Lepper (1979), *Biased Assimilation and Attitude Polarization*
- Edwards (classical urn-experiment reference cited in the proposal; manual bibliographic confirmation recommended)

## Algorithms, Trust, and Human--Computer Interaction

- Parasuraman and Riley (1997), *Humans and Automation: Use, Misuse, Disuse, Abuse*
- Lee and See (2004), *Trust in Automation: Designing for Appropriate Reliance*
- Dietvorst, Simmons, and Massey (2015), *Algorithm Aversion*
- Logg, Minson, and Moore (2019), *Algorithm Appreciation*
- Reeves and Nass (1996), *The Media Equation*
- Nass and Moon (2000), *Machines and Mindlessness*
- Epley, Waytz, and Cacioppo (2007), *On Seeing Human*
- Waytz, Cacioppo, and Epley (2010), *Who Sees Human?*

## Recommender Systems and Generative AI

- Resnick and Varian (1997), *Recommender Systems*
- Adomavicius and Tuzhilin (2005), *Toward the Next Generation of Recommender Systems*
- Bozdag (2013), *Bias in Algorithmic Filtering and Personalization*
- Vaswani et al. (2017), *Attention Is All You Need*
- Brown et al. (2020), *Language Models are Few-Shot Learners*
- Ouyang et al. (2022), *Training Language Models to Follow Instructions with Human Feedback*
- Bender et al. (2021), *On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots*

## Narrative, Epistemic Agency, and Medical AI

- Green and Brock (2000), *The Role of Transportation in the Persuasiveness of Public Narratives*
- Woodruff and Hewitt (2026), *Epistemic Agency in the Age of Large Language Models*
- Qi and Pan (2026), *Epistemic and Ethical Limits of Large Language Models in Evidence-Based Medicine*
